    Chargeback Management

    Chargebacks are constantly becoming a major threat to online business, so don’t let chargebacks affect your business bottom line. As a business owner, you might be probably thinking one chargeback doesn’t sound too bad. However, it can quickly add up and impact the bottom line of any growing business and lead to closure of merchant accounts.

    What is a chargeback? Chargeback in simpler terms is a dispute raised by a card holder (via their issuing bank) against the merchant, to initiate a refund for a purchase made on their card. In the chargeback process, the burden of proof lies with the merchant. The merchant will be given an opportunity to provide supporting documentation to prove the legitimacy of the transaction. If the merchant is successful, the transaction is credited back to his account. If the merchant is unsuccessful, or does not respond in a timely fashion, they will be financially responsible for returning funds to the consumer who filed the dispute. Reasons for Chargeback There are many reasons why a cardholder may dispute the transaction and cause a chargeback to your merchant account.
  • Fraudulent transactions
  • Credit Not Processed
    • Cancelled Recurring
  • Duplicate Processing
  • Service Not Provided / Merchandise Not Received
  • Transaction Not Recognized
